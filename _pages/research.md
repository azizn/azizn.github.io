## Research Clusters

<div style="margin-top: 30px;">

### 5G/6G Wireless Communications & Signal Processing
<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #667eea 0%, #764ba2 100%); padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; color: white; margin-bottom: 30px;">
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">This is my most active research area, focusing on next-generation wireless communication systems. Key topics include:</p>

<ul style="margin-bottom: 0;">
<li><strong>PAPR Reduction</strong>: Developing hybrid algorithms (PTS, SLM, genetic algorithms, neural networks) to reduce Peak-to-Average Power Ratio in OFDM, NOMA, OTFS, and FBMC waveforms</li>
<li><strong>Signal Detection</strong>: Deep learning-based signal detectors (LSTM, Bi-LSTM, DNN, CNN) for massive MIMO systems</li>
<li><strong>Spectrum Sensing</strong>: Cognitive radio techniques and energy detection methods for 5G/6G networks</li>
<li><strong>NOMA/OTFS/MIMO Systems</strong>: Non-orthogonal multiple access and orthogonal time frequency space modulation for beyond 5G</li>
</ul>
</div>

**Selected Publications:**
- Kumar, A., Gaur, N., & Nanthaamornphong, A. (2025). Enhanced PAPR reduction in NOMA systems using modified SLM and PTS techniques. *Open Engineering*. [(doi)](https://doi.org/10.1515/eng-2025-0153)
- Kumar, A., Nanthaamornphong, A., Gaur, N., & Masud, M. (2025). Low complexity hybrid algorithm for improving PAPR, BER, and PSD in OTFS. *Scientific Reports*. [(doi)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-26748-w)
- Kumar, A., Nanthaamornphong, A., & Masud, M. (2025). RNN-Bi-LSTM spectrum sensing algorithm for NOMA waveform. *Scientific Reports*. [(doi)](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-025-16414-6)

### Optical & Visible Light Communications
<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #f093fb 0%, #f5576c 100%); padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; color: white; margin-bottom: 30px;">
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">Research on optical communication systems for high-speed data transmission:</p>

<ul style="margin-bottom: 0;">
<li><strong>Optical OFDM/NOMA</strong>: Signal processing techniques for optical waveforms with high-order QAM (256-QAM, 512-QAM, 1024-QAM)</li>
<li><strong>VLC Applications</strong>: Visible light communication systems for indoor positioning and data transmission</li>
<li><strong>Free Space Optical (FSO)</strong>: Performance analysis under diverse channel conditions</li>
<li><strong>Underwater Communications</strong>: Optical network frameworks for underwater applications</li>
</ul>
</div>

**Selected Publications:**
- Kumar, A. et al. (2025). Efficient hybrid signal detection for optical NOMA with 512-QAM in visible light communication. *Journal of Optical Communications*.
- Hiremath, M. et al. (2025). A hybrid deep learning and companding technique for distortion-resilient optical NOMA VLC with 1024-QAM modulation. *Journal of Optical Communications*.

### AI/ML for Medical Imaging & Healthcare
<div style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #4facfe 0%, #00f2fe 100%); padding: 20px; border-radius: 10px; color: white; margin-bottom: 30px;">
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px;">Applying deep learning and machine learning techniques for medical diagnosis and healthcare applications:</p>

<ul style="margin-bottom: 0;">

</ul>
